# community-engagement

How NUNU generates community engagement, activation, and Instagram content for a Frank Developments
project. Same Critical Rule as everything NUNU does: **grounded in the real project, never generic**
(run [[../brand-fidelity-check/SKILL|brand-fidelity-check]] on every output). Yana is the voice; NUNU
gives her a strong, specific starting set she shapes and posts.

## When to use
- Yana needs: Instagram story-post ideas, community engagement angles, or event/activation concepts for a project.

## Inputs (from the project's folder in nunu-wiki)
- Project guidelines + brand voice + design reference.
- Who the community/audience actually is (buyer profile, locale, stage of the project).
- Any real hooks: upcoming milestones, the neighbourhood, amenities, partners, events.

## What NUNU produces

### Instagram story posts
- Ideas tied to a *real* project moment or detail — not "5 tips" filler. Each idea = the angle + a one-line caption in Frank's voice + a visual/asset suggestion (drawn from the design reference) + an interaction hook (poll / question / countdown / link sticker) with a purpose.
- Sequences, not one-offs: a story arc across a few frames (tease → reveal → CTA) beats isolated posts.
- Never generic engagement-bait. The interaction must serve the project (gauge buyer interest, drive registrations, surface a real question).

### Community engagement (replies, DMs)
- Suggested voice + posture for replying to comments/DMs in Frank's tone (warm, considered, never canned).
- Draft responses to *common* buyer questions — but flag anything factual (price, availability, timeline) for Yana to confirm before sending. Never invent project facts in a public reply.

### Events / activations
- Concepts tied to the project + locale: what would genuinely draw the target buyer, what moment it marks, and a shortlist of external stakeholders to reach out to (partners, venues, local businesses, media) with why each fits.
- For each concept: the idea, who it's for, the hook, effort/impact read, and the outreach list. Ideation Yana takes and runs.

## Output
- A tight, ranked idea set (not a brainstorm dump) — each item specific enough that it could only be this project, with the caption/voice ready for Yana to refine.

## Gotchas
- **Specific beats clever.** A real detail about this project's locale outperforms a witty generic line.
- **Never fabricate** availability, price, or timeline in any public-facing or DM reply — flag for Yana.
- **Match the platform.** Stories are fast, visual, interactive — not mini-brochures.
- **Ranked, not endless.** Give Yana the best few, with a reason, not 30 to sift.
- **KPI-aware:** tie ideas back to what moves Yana's numbers (registrations, engagement, buyer intent) — she's gunning to run the department.
